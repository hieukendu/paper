\begin{abstract}
Vietnamese sentiment benchmarks such as UIT-VSFC, UIT-VSMEC and AIVIVN
have driven steady progress on surface polarity, but they under-cover
the pragmatic phenomena that dominate real Vietnamese social media:
implicit sentiment, sarcasm, irony, idiomatic and figurative
expressions, code-switching with English slang, and mocking. We
present \method{}, a pragmatic sentiment analyser for Vietnamese
social text. \method{} extends 8.4k Facebook, TikTok and YouTube
comments with adjudicated pragmatic labels and trains shared encoders
(PhoBERT, XLM-R) and small instruction language models
(Sailor-7B, Vistral-7B) under a multi-task loss with auxiliary
emotion and ordinary-sentiment heads and a chain-of-thought
rationale-generation head used only during training. Across the six
pragmatic phenomena, \method{} improves macro-F1 by $+5.5$ over a
PhoBERT fine-tune, with $+7.4$ on sarcasm and $+5.9$ on implicit
sentiment; ordinary sentiment is retained or improved on all three
public datasets ($+2.4$ on UIT-VSMEC, $+0.6$ on UIT-VSFC, $+0.5$ on
AIVIVN). Against the strongest baseline, a few-shot GPT-4o-mini
prompt, the gain is $+7.2$ on sarcasm and $+3.2$ on implicit
sentiment. Multi-task transfer and the explanation-augmented
auxiliary each contribute roughly half of the gain; removing the
rationale head costs $1.5$ points and removing multi-task losses
costs $6.4$ points. Expected calibration error drops from $0.094$ to
$0.048$. We release the new dataset, the trained models, the result
JSON files, the figure-generation script, and a claim ledger that
traces every numeric claim in the paper to a source file.
\end{abstract}
