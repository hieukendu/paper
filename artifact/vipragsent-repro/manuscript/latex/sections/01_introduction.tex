\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Vietnamese social-media NLP must contend with user-generated language whose form and communicative function can differ from more standardized text.  This motivates evaluation that is sensitive to pragmatic phenomena as well as to polarity classification.  Vietnamese social-media pretraining is therefore relevant context for the present study, as illustrated by ViSoBERT \citep{nguyen-etal-2023-visobert}.  We study a fixed, adjudicated evaluation setting that combines a documented social-media source component with author-created, context-augmented figurative-language derivatives, and compare recorded systems under a common protocol.  This keeps the paper focused on what the retained artifacts establish, rather than treating the entire corpus as homogeneous social-media text or claiming a public release of the underlying raw text.

The central gap is correspondingly narrow.  Existing Vietnamese resources and pretrained-model work provide important task, domain, and modeling context, but they do not substitute for a controlled comparison on the pragmatic phenomena operationalized here.  ViPragSent defines implicit sentiment, sarcasm, irony, idiom/figurative language, code-switching, and mocking as six distinct evaluation targets, while retaining polarity and emotion as auxiliary labels rather than recasting them as pragmatic phenomena.  The aim is not to claim general coverage of pragmatics in Vietnamese social media.  It is to make the behavior of several system families inspectable on this specified label construction, with annotation, governance, and reporting boundaries made explicit.  The labels are measurement targets for this study, not a taxonomy of all pragmatic behavior, a claim about cultural prevalence, or a guarantee that performance will transfer to a different corpus.  In particular, external sentiment and emotion benchmarks remain evaluation-only resources, separate from the constructed corpus and its private, non-commercial source-text boundary.

% TRACE: I-C02 | answer/results/main_pragmatic.json; configs/experiments_q1.yaml | system-group inventory | exact locked RQ and comparison boundary
Our research question is: \emph{On an adjudicated Vietnamese-language test set covering six pragmatic phenomena and including a documented social-media source component, how do standard Vietnamese/multilingual encoders, 7B instruction-tuned models, prompted API baselines, and a multi-task encoder compare; and what trade-off does the recorded ablation study show between pragmatic detection and an external ordinary-task diagnostic?}  This question fixes the comparison boundary to four groups: PhoBERT/XLM-R encoders, Sailor-7B/Vistral-7B QLoRA SFT, GPT-4.1-mini zero-shot and 8-shot prompting, and the ViPragSent multi-task encoder.  The comparison is evaluative rather than a proposed-model-superiority test.  It also distinguishes the three recorded seeds for learned encoders and 7B systems from the single recorded runs for prompted API baselines, and treats the ablation as a separate, qualified diagnostic rather than a replacement for the main comparison.  Interpretations are restricted to the recorded systems, fixed data, and stored evaluations; they do not project an ordering to systems, phenomena, or deployment settings outside that scope.

The paper makes the following evidence-bounded contributions:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item % TRACE: C1 | answer/data_provenance/gold_build_report.json; answer/results/annotation_agreement.json | 12,000 records and six phenomena | no public raw-text release
  It defines and evaluates six pragmatic phenomena in 12,000 adjudicated Vietnamese-language records, comprising 10,000 retained local social-media export records and 2,000 author-created, context-augmented derivatives based on VIVID idiom/proverb materials, alongside polarity and emotion annotations while preserving the private research-data boundary.
  \item % TRACE: C2 | answer/results/main_pragmatic.json; answer/run_manifests/ | recorded systems and seed/run scope | comparative evaluation only
  It provides a comparative evaluation across the four defined system groups, with learned encoders and 7B systems represented by recorded seeds and prompted API baselines qualified as single recorded runs.
  \item % TRACE: C3 | answer/results/main_pragmatic.json | vistral_7b_sft=82.83; vipragsent_full=73.75 | strongest recorded system; no universal superiority claim
  It reports the main comparison transparently: Vistral-7B SFT is the strongest recorded evaluated system at 82.83 macro pragmatic F1, whereas ViPragSent records 73.75; the proposed multi-task encoder is therefore not the best-performing recorded system.
  \item % TRACE: C4 | answer/results/multitask_ablation.json | single seed 20260520 | observed trade-off; no causal attribution
  It analyzes a recorded single-seed pragmatic-versus-external-ordinary-task trade-off between the full multi-task configuration and its no-multitask reference, without attributing the observed difference causally to the auxiliary objectives.
  \item % TRACE: C5 | answer/results/claim_ledger.csv; answer/reproducibility/{verification_manifest,artifact_registry}.json | artifact chain | traceability only; not a fresh rerun
  It links reported comparisons to prediction files, run manifests, result hashes, and pinned external model archives, establishing artifact-level traceability rather than an independent reproduction or fresh rerun.
\end{enumerate}
