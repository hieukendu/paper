\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

% TRACE: S7-C01 | manuscript/PAPER_PLAN.md | Locked research question; answer/results/main_pragmatic.json | $.systems; answer/results/multitask_ablation.json | $.systems
This study asked how four system groups compare on a defined Vietnamese pragmatic-sentiment evaluation with a documented social-media source component and what the recorded ablation shows about pragmatic detection and the external ordinary-task diagnostic.  Within that setting, the strongest recorded systems are the 7B SFT baselines, with Vistral-7B SFT leading the stored main comparison.  The comparison therefore does not support a model-superiority claim for ViPragSent.  Instead, ViPragSent contributes a traceable multi-task encoder evaluation whose ablation records a qualified pragmatic-versus-external-ordinary-task trade-off relative to its no-multitask reference.  The result is an empirical characterization of the retained systems, labels, and test data, rather than a claim about all Vietnamese pragmatic language or all future deployments.

Future work may extend the ablation to multiple seeds, evaluate ViSoBERT, establish safe data-access arrangements, and develop permission-safe qualitative analysis.  These are future extensions, not completed evidence in the present study.
