# ViPragSent Annotation Guideline v1

**Status:** author-team-defined project protocol  
**Recorded:** 2026-07-18  
**Scope:** six binary pragmatic labels in adjudicated Vietnamese social-media comments

## Evidence boundary

This document records the annotation protocol defined by the project team and supplied during manuscript preparation.  It is a project-specific operational framework, not a transcription of, or a claim of adherence to, a particular external literary source.  Any literature-specific attribution in the manuscript requires separately verified sources.

The definitions below are interpretive criteria, not deterministic lexical rules.  Annotators apply them together with contextual judgment.  A comment may receive multiple pragmatic labels.  The protocol contains no raw social-media examples because the source text remains private, non-commercial research data.

## Pragmatic labels

| Repository label | Vietnamese gloss | Annotation criterion |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `implicit_sentiment` | Cảm xúc/thái độ hàm ẩn | Assign when a comment expresses emotion or evaluation without stating it directly through explicit emotion words, such that interpretation relies on context, metaphor, comparison, rhetorical questioning, or another indirect formulation. |
| `sarcasm` | Mỉa mai/châm biếm | Assign when the surface tone is opposite to the writer's intended stance.  This commonly involves a polarity reversal, such as positive wording used to convey a negative stance, or the reverse. |
| `irony` | Trớ trêu/nghịch lý | Assign when the comment presents an incongruity between what is expected and what actually occurs.  Unlike sarcasm, irony does not require a complete polarity reversal. |
| `idiom_figurative` | Thành ngữ/ngôn ngữ hình tượng | Assign when a comment contains a fixed Vietnamese idiom or a figurative expression whose meaning extends beyond a literal reading, including metaphor, personification, hyperbole, or figurative comparison. |
| `code_switching` | Chuyển mã/ngôn ngữ pha trộn | Assign when a Vietnamese comment contains at least one English content word.  Do not assign solely for common abbreviations or widely borrowed terms such as `OK` or `Internet`. |
| `mocking` | Chế giễu/giễu cợt | Assign when the comment ridicules, demeans, or turns an individual, group, or action into an object of ridicule.  This label may co-occur with sarcasm. |

## Annotation and adjudication procedure

1. Reviewers apply the six definitions with contextual, interpretive judgment; no single cue word is sufficient by itself.
2. Labels are binary and independent, so more than one pragmatic label may be assigned to a comment.
3. The third reviewer is the fixed adjudicator.  After review, the adjudicator selects the final labels that define the experimental gold split.
4. Agreement statistics therefore describe a post-adjudication workflow.  They are not evidence of an independent blinded third-rater study.

## Change control

Future revisions must receive a new version identifier and must not be used to retroactively reinterpret the fixed experimental gold labels without a separately documented study.
